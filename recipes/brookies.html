Butter Soft Brookies

Absolutely! Here's a recipe for a decadent keto-friendly brookie that combines the best of both worlds: chewy brownie and crispy cookie in one delightful treat.

Ingredients:

For the cookie layer:

  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup melted butter or coconut oil
  • 1/3 cup granulated erythritol or monk fruit sweetener
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (optional)

For the brownie layer:

  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup melted butter or coconut oil
  • 1/3 cup granulated erythritol or monk fruit sweetener
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup chopped sugar-free chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. Make the cookie layer: In a medium bowl, whisk together almond flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together melted butter or coconut oil, erythritol or monk fruit sweetener, egg, and vanilla extract. Pour wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Fold in chopped nuts, if using.
  4. Make the brownie layer: In another medium bowl, whisk together almond flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Repeat step 3, whisking together melted butter or coconut oil, erythritol or monk fruit sweetener, egg, and vanilla extract. Pour wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Fold in chopped sugar-free chocolate chips, if using.
  6. Spread the cookie layer evenly across the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Dollop spoonfuls of the brownie batter over the cookie layer, creating a marbled effect.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with moist crumbs. Let cool completely in the pan before cutting into squares.

Tips:

  • For extra fudgy brownies, bake for a few minutes less.
  • You can use other nut flours in place of almond flour, such as hazelnut flour or coconut flour. Just be sure to adjust the other ingredients accordingly.
  • If you don't have erythritol or monk fruit sweetener, you can use stevia, but be careful because it is much sweeter than sugar.
  • You can also add other keto-friendly ingredients to the brookies, such as chopped dark chocolate, peanut butter chips, or espresso powder.

Enjoy your delicious and guilt-free keto-friendly brookies! They're sure to satisfy your sweet tooth and keep you on track with your keto goals.
